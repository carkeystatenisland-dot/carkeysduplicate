# Dealer vs. Mobile Locksmith for Car Key Duplication in NYC — Honest Comparison

When you need a spare car key or find yourself completely locked out, you typically have two choices: go to the dealership or call a mobile automotive locksmith. 

## Speed: Same-Day vs. Waiting Days

- **The Dealership:** If the dealer doesn't have your specific key blank in stock, they must order it. This can take 2 to 5 business days. You must also schedule an appointment for the service department to program it.
- **Mobile Locksmith:** We carry hundreds of key blanks and fobs in our vans. In 95% of cases, we provide same-day service, often arriving within 30-60 minutes and finishing the job right then and there.

## Cost Breakdown

Dealerships are notorious for high markups on parts and labor. A replacement smart key at a dealership can easily exceed $350. Mobile locksmiths perform the same service, using the same OEM-grade diagnostic tools, often for $150 to $200. You are paying for the key and the programming, not the dealership's overhead.

## Quality: OEM vs. Aftermarket

A common myth is that locksmiths only use "cheap" aftermarket keys. The truth is, reputable locksmiths use high-quality aftermarket keys containing the exact same transponder chips (like NXP or Texas Instruments) as the OEM keys. We also offer official OEM fobs if preferred. The functionality is identical.

## The Convenience Factor

In NYC, getting a car to a dealership when you have no working keys means paying for a tow truck. A mobile locksmith comes directly to your location, whether you are parked on a street in Queens or in a tight garage in Manhattan.

## When Should You Choose the Dealer?

There are rare situations where the dealer is the better option:
1. Your car is brand new (e.g., 2025 model) and the programming software hasn't been released to the aftermarket yet.
2. The key replacement is fully covered under a specific dealership warranty or recall.
