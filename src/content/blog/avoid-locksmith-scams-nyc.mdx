# How to Avoid Locksmith Scams in NYC

When you're locked out of your car or desperately need a spare key, you are in a vulnerable position. Unfortunately, the locksmith industry in New York City has its share of scammers ready to take advantage.

## The Bait-and-Switch Pricing Scam

The most common scam starts with a suspiciously low quote. You call a number from a Google ad, and they tell you the service call is only "$15" or "$25." 

When the "technician" arrives, they will suddenly claim that your car has a "high security" system, or that the programming is more complex than expected. That $15 quote rapidly inflates to $300 or $400. They will demand cash and apply high-pressure tactics.

**How to avoid this:** Legitimate automotive locksmiths will ask for the Year, Make, and Model of your vehicle *before* quoting. They know exactly what technology is inside your car and will provide a solid price range over the phone.

## Fake Google My Business Listings

Many scammers operate out of out-of-state call centers. They create dozens of fake Google Maps listings with generic names and fake local addresses. When you call, a dispatcher routes the job to an unlicensed subcontractor. 

**How to avoid this:** Ask specific local questions. A real NYC locksmith knows the boroughs. Look for professionally branded vans and technicians wearing uniforms.

## Licensing in New York State

In New York City, locksmiths are required by the Department of Consumer and Worker Protection (DCWP) to hold a valid license. 
- Ask to see their license when they arrive.
- Make sure their vehicle is clearly marked with their business name.
